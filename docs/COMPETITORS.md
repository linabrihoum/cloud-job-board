# Competitors

Researched July 2026.

## Inspiration

### Career Vault — https://careervault.io/
- **What it is:** general remote job board (software, design, sales,
  healthcare...), not cloud-specific. Flat $199 per job post.
- **Does well:** simple flat pricing, clean pitch ("Land a remote job.
  Live anywhere").
- **Weaknesses seen:** only a couple of featured jobs on the homepage,
  placeholder stock photos instead of real company logos, and **no posting
  dates on listings** — the exact staleness problem this project targets.

### Space Crew — https://spacecrew.com/
- **What it is:** the model for a niche board done right, but for the space
  industry. 7,000+ listings, clean categories, a blog for industry
  credibility.
- **Does well:** strong distinctive design (the visual reference for this
  project), and **email alerts** (daily/weekly/monthly digests of new
  matching jobs) as its retention engine.
- **Lesson:** email alerts matter more than expected — promoted to the
  first post-MVP feature on our roadmap because of this research.

## Direct competitors (found in research)

### DevOps Projects HQ — https://devopsprojectshq.com/
- **What it is:** the closest existing site to this idea. DevOps/SRE/
  Platform Engineering only, 5,000+ listings, daily updates, filters for
  role/location/salary/level.
- **Does well:** freshness, niche focus, salary ranges.
- **Weaknesses:** **charges job seekers** — most listings sit behind a
  paywall with no transparent pricing. Engineers resent paying to see jobs.
- **Our wedge against them:** free for job seekers, always.

### LearnXOps — https://www.learnxops.com/
- **What it is:** a newsletter with an attached board of ~1,200 curated
  cloud/DevOps/MLOps jobs.
- **Does well:** curation, credibility with the community.
- **Weaknesses:** the board is a side feature of a newsletter, not a
  destination site.

## The generalists (LinkedIn, Indeed, We Work Remotely, RemoteOK)

Huge volume, but cloud/infra roles are buried in noise, duplicates are
rampant, and there's no curation for this niche. Their free feeds (RemoteOK
API, We Work Remotely and Remotive RSS) were considered as sources, but
their terms require linking back to their own listing pages — which
conflicts with this board's rule that "Apply" always lands on the company's
own posting. Jobs are sourced from company hiring systems instead.

## The gap, in one sentence

Nobody in this niche is simultaneously **free for job seekers, visibly
fresh, and well-designed** — that's the position this board takes.
